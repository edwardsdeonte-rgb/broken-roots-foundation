Our Story

Why We're Here

Losing a parent or guardian is one of the most profound experiences a person can go through — whether you're 8, 28, or 58. For younger people, it can bring anxiety, financial hardship, and a loneliness that's hard to explain to peers who haven't been there. For adults, it can quietly reshape everything — your identity, your relationships, your sense of direction.

The truth is, there's no right age to grieve and no timeline for healing. The Broken Roots Foundation was created to be a place for all of it — the young person figuring out life without their parent, and the grown adult who still reaches for the phone to call someone who's no longer there. This is a community for anyone who has lost a piece of their roots.

"In 2016, my four siblings and I lost our mother to cardiovascular heart disease. We had each other — and it was still one of the hardest things we've ever been through. That experience showed us something we'll never forget: everyone deserves a community when life falls apart. This foundation is that community. Whether you have people around you or no one at all, you belong here."

— The Founder

What We Do

Our Programs

Every person's grief is unique. We meet you where you are — whatever your age, wherever you are.

💛

Emotional Support

One-on-one and group grief sessions with compassionate counselors and peers. Virtual and in-person options available.

🤝

Financial Assistance

Help covering funeral costs, burial services, and related expenses so families can focus on healing.

🌱

Community

Support groups, memorial gatherings, and a safe space to share your story and be heard by people who understand.

🩷

Therapy Access

Connecting grieving individuals with licensed therapists and covering costs when we can.

🧒

Youth Programs

Age-appropriate workshops and creative expression for children and teens processing the loss of a parent.

🕊️

Mentorship

Pairing those who've walked through loss with those still finding their way — at any age.
